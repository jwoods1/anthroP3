Tahitian Business Culture

Tahitian Learnability

When doing business in Tahiti is has a informal atmosphere. Most of the literature will be in French, although English is understood in some business connected with tourism. The culture is layed back and easy going. Most office hours are Mon-Fri 0800-1200 and 1330-1730, Sat 0800-1200.

Business Etiquette

Going to the islands to do business has its major conflicts. On one hand you think layed back and relaxed, but on the other you think that business should be surious business. With the French Polynesian Islands they tend to keep it more relaxed. You will want to aviod aggressive negotiations. You should plan to spend some time waiting becuase punctuality is not imporant. You should stay relaxed in meetings and maintain eye contact. These are just a few tips to keep you on the upper hand in your next meeting.

Language of Business.

French is the main language of business, although English is understood in main tourist areas. It is in your best intrest to learn some French and Tahitian terms. Here are a few.

    Tahitian:

  • Iaorana (E-yo-or-ahna) = Hello

  • Mauruuru (ma-rou-rou) = Thank you

  • Nana (nah-nah) = Goodbye

  • Maeva (ma-ay-va) = Welcome
