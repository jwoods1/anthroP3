Tahitian Culture

When you first get to one of the islands you are treated like an old friend.

Tahitian Celebrations

In the month of July Tahitians celebrate Tiurai festivities. These actives take place in the middle of July. They celebrate Polyenesian warriors and Tahitian culutre. The festivities include parades, athletic competitions, dancing, pagentry, and cultural events. They also have song and dance competitions.

Tahitian Cuisine

The food in Tahiti is one of the best ways to experiance the taste of culture. The main dishes consist of fish and other types of seafood. An example of this is poisson cru, raw fish marinated in coconut milk and lime juice. Other popular Tahitian foods consist of tropical fruits, and pork.

Most foods in Tahiti are traditionally eaten with the fingers. Locals are accustomed to tourists requesting a fork or spoon. Food in traditionally cooked in an ahimaa, or hole dug into the ground.

Way of Life

Tahitians are a very kind, respectful and generous people. The philosophy of the people "not to worry", is the Tahitian way of life. They are very warm and welcoming to everyone that is kind to them.
