Getting To and Around Tahiti

Planes Trains Automobiles and the ins and outs of travel in and out of Tahiti

Flying around Tahiti

Air Tahiti has several regular flights between 46 islands out of Tahiti. It takes about 10 minutes to travel to sister island Moorea. Air Moorea makes the short flight several times a day. There are several other options such as charter flights and helicopter rides.

Getting around by Boat

One of the more leisure ways to travel to sister islands is by ferrie. The ferrie or catamaran takes about a half hour to get to Moorea from Tahiti. Other options are avalible such as boat rentals or yacht charters. The Crewed luxury yachts service the islands will enable you to be pampered.

Cruises are also a major form of travel to Tahiti. There are serveral cruise lines that come in to Tahiti.

And then the Bus

The bus is the most common form of public transportaion on the island. The tahitians call it "le Truck". Most buses are old open-air bus with wooden passenger cabins. The will stop on the side of the street and serve different cities. It is about $2 US dallors per person. Most bus routes will end up in the center of downtown by the market.

Other forms of travel

Like most cities in other parts of the world you will have your other forms of travel. The other is concidered private cars, biking or scooters. On Tahiti most cars that you can rent will be stickshift and the average cost per day is $90 US dallors. There is a lot of bike rental opertunities and they are much cheaper then the car. Scooters are a also a option, but most discurage against scooters. The reason behind this is the danger of crashing the the rate that it accures. Most major touring sites recommend to stay away from scooters so I will also suggest this.
